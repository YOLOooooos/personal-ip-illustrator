# Style System

Use this reference when the user has not already chosen a style, or when the chosen style needs a clear generation contract.

## Style Contract

For every style, define:

- **Rendering**: line, flat vector, 3D, paper cut, ink, pixel, editorial collage, etc.
- **Palette**: dominant colors, accent colors, background rules, forbidden colors.
- **Shape language**: geometric, organic, soft, sharp, modular, hand-drawn.
- **Texture**: clean, grain, clay, paper, glass, metal, marker, watercolor.
- **Composition**: centered, diagrammatic, poster-like, sequential, full-bleed, white-space heavy.
- **Typography**: no text, tiny labels, handwritten labels, UI-like labels, title-card text.
- **Forbidden traits**: elements that would make the style generic or inconsistent.

## Recommended Style Directions

Use these as starting points, not as rigid categories.

1. **Minimal line editorial**
   - Clean line art, restrained color, large white space, strong metaphor.
   - Best for essays, systems thinking, technical analysis, knowledge posts.

2. **Flat geometric character**
   - Simple vector shapes, stable silhouette, high readability at small sizes.
   - Best for social posts, SaaS explainers, creator brands.

3. **Soft 3D clay**
   - Rounded forms, tactile surfaces, soft lighting, toy-like recognizability.
   - Best for friendly education, product demos, accessible technical content.

4. **Ink and paper notebook**
   - Hand-drawn marks, off-white paper, small annotations, sketchbook energy.
   - Best for thinking notes, research breakdowns, learning journals.

5. **Editorial collage**
   - Cutout shapes, symbolic props, layered composition, magazine-like pacing.
   - Best for opinion essays, cultural commentary, business analysis.

6. **Pixel or low-res icon world**
   - Pixel grid, compact scenes, game-like logic, limited palette.
   - Best for developer content, indie products, playful technical explainers.

7. **Sticker mascot system**
   - Bold outline, expressive pose, transparent or simple background.
   - Best for thumbnails, comment stickers, community identity, reaction images.

8. **Diagrammatic isometric**
   - Small environments, system maps, modular objects, readable process scenes.
   - Best for workflows, architecture explanations, product operations.

9. **Monochrome plus one accent**
   - Mostly black, gray, or neutral line work with one signature color.
   - Best for strong personal branding and fast recognition across posts.

10. **Surreal concept metaphor**
   - Impossible objects, symbolic scale shifts, dreamlike but controlled scenes.
   - Best for abstract concepts, contrarian essays, mental models.

## Selection Heuristics

- Prefer line/editorial styles for dense articles.
- Prefer sticker or flat systems for high-frequency social publishing.
- Prefer isometric or diagrammatic styles when the content often explains workflows.
- Prefer 3D clay only when friendliness matters more than precision.
- Avoid a style that requires too many details to keep the IP recognizable.
